## Overview

The **Brokerage Management** domain is the root organizational context of the Yosef platform. It governs the two-level hierarchy through which insurance is distributed:

```
Brokerage  — a large insurance company (root organization)
└── Broker — a sub-company granted access to sell via Yosef
    └── Insured — the policyholder / customer
```

A **Brokerage** owns one or more **Brokers**. Brokers cannot have sub-Brokers — the hierarchy is fixed at two levels.

An **Insured** is registered once at the **Brokerage** level and may be served by multiple Brokers within that Brokerage (N:M via `BrokerInsured`). This prevents duplicate records when the same person has policies under different Brokers, and gives the Brokerage a consolidated view of all its Insureds.

**Projects** are insurance product lines (e.g., Bike Insurance, Ship Insurance) scoped to a Brokerage.

## Ubiquitous Language

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Brokerage** | Root organization. The large insurance company that owns the platform contract. |
| **Broker** | Sub-organization operating under a Brokerage. Can onboard Insureds and request Quotations. |
| **Insured** | The customer (natural or legal person) who holds or will hold an insurance Policy. |
| **Project** | An insurance product line managed by a Brokerage (e.g., "Bike Insurance"). Scopes Quotations and Policies. |
