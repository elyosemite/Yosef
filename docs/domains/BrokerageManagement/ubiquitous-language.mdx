---
dictionary:
  - id: Brokerage
    name: Brokerage
    summary: 'The root organization — a large insurance company that holds the platform contract and owns Brokers.'
    description: |
      A Brokerage is the top-level entity in the Yosef hierarchy. It represents a large insurance company
      that operates on the platform and delegates sales and customer management to sub-companies called Brokers.

      Key behaviors:
      - Can be created, renamed, and archived (soft-deleted)
      - Owns one or more Brokers
      - Owns one or more Projects (insurance product lines)
      - Cannot be deleted once it has active Brokers or Policies

      Previously called "Organization" in the codebase — rename on sight.
    icon: BuildingOffice2

  - id: Broker
    name: Broker
    summary: 'A sub-company operating under a Brokerage, granted access to sell insurance via Yosef.'
    description: |
      A Broker is registered under exactly one Brokerage and is responsible for onboarding Insureds and
      requesting Quotations on their behalf.

      Key behaviors:
      - Belongs to exactly one Brokerage (modeled as entity inside the Brokerage aggregate)
      - Cannot have sub-Brokers (two-level hierarchy is fixed)
      - Can be associated to Insureds (the association is owned by the Insured aggregate)
      - Can request Quotations for Insureds within the Projects owned by its Brokerage
    icon: BuildingStorefront

  - id: Insured
    name: Insured
    summary: 'The customer (natural person or legal entity) who holds or will hold an insurance Policy.'
    description: |
      An Insured is registered once at the Brokerage level and may be served by multiple Brokers (N:M).
      They are the subject of Quotations, Policies, and Claims.

      Key behaviors:
      - Belongs to exactly one Brokerage (registered there, no duplication)
      - Associated to one or more Brokers within that Brokerage via a BrokerInsured join
      - Can have multiple active Policies across different Projects
      - Can file Claims against their Policies
      - A Brokerage has a consolidated view of all its Insureds across all Brokers
      - Typed via InsuredType discriminator: Individual (CPF) or Corporate (CNPJ)

      Do not use "Client" or "Customer" — the canonical term is Insured.
    icon: UserCircle

  - id: Project
    name: Project
    summary: 'An insurance product line owned by a Brokerage (e.g., Bike Insurance, Ship Insurance).'
    description: |
      A Project scopes the type of insurance being sold. Each Quotation and Policy is associated with a Project.

      Examples:
      - "Bike Insurance" project
      - "Ship Insurance" project
      - "Aircraft Insurance" project
    icon: FolderOpen

  - id: Quotation
    name: Quotation
    summary: 'A pricing proposal for an insurance product, tied to an Insured and a Project.'
    description: |
      A Quotation is created by a Broker on behalf of an Insured for a specific Project. It contains
      the proposed coverage terms and premium. A Quotation may be approved, rejected, or expire.
    icon: DocumentText

  - id: Policy
    name: Policy
    summary: 'The final insurance contract issued from an approved Quotation.'
    description: |
      A Policy is the binding legal contract between the Insured and the Brokerage. It is generated
      from an approved Quotation and defines coverage, premium, and validity period.
    icon: ShieldCheck

  - id: Claim
    name: Claim
    summary: 'A formal request by an Insured to receive benefits under an active Policy.'
    description: |
      The full Claim lifecycle: FNOL (First Notice of Loss) → Investigation → Assessment → Approval/Rejection → Settlement.
    icon: ClipboardDocumentList

  - id: DomainEvent
    name: Domain Event
    summary: 'A record that something meaningful happened in the domain. Always named in past tense.'
    description: |
      Domain Events are the primary communication mechanism between services in Yosef.
      Examples: BrokerageCreatedEvent, BrokerRegisteredEvent, InsuredRegisteredEvent.
      They are published via the Outbox Pattern and consumed by EventProcessor.
    icon: BoltSlash
